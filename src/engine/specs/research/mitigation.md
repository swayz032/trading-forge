### 1. InnerCircleTrader.net — [Master the Mitigation Block ICT and Learn to Trade it](https://innercircletrader.net/tutorials/ict-mitigation-block-explained/)
- **Definition:** A mitigation block is an order block that temporarily reverses price but FAILS to continue the current trend (no new HH or LL). Price then reverses and pushes past the block to create a market structure shift.
- **Bearish MB formation:** Price makes HH, pulls back to HL. The HL fails to produce a new HH. Price breaks below the HL, confirming BOS and creating a LH. The zone from the broken swing low to the lower high is the bearish mitigation block.
- **Bullish MB formation:** Price makes LL, retraces to LH. The LH fails to drive price lower. Price breaks above the LH, confirming structure shift and forming HL. The zone is the bullish mitigation block.
- **Key distinction from breaker:** A breaker block raids liquidity (makes new HH/LL) before reversing. A mitigation block DOES NOT take out the prior swing — it fails before doing so.
- **Entry:** Sell limit at bottom of bearish MB; buy limit at top of bullish MB on retest.

### 3. OpoFinance — [ICT Breaker Block vs Mitigation Block](https://blog.opofinance.com/en/ict-breaker-block-vs-mitigation-block/)
- **Breaker vs Mitigation key difference:** A breaker block creates a HH/LL before the OB is broken. A mitigation block fails to break above the swing high (or below swing low) before shifting structure.
- **Reclaimed OBs:** When breaker or mitigation blocks fail, they revert to "Reclaimed Order Blocks."
- **Trading approach:** Both are traded on retest, but the formation context is different.

## Consensus Rules (all sources agree)

### Definition
A Mitigation Block is an **order block that FAILS to continue the prevailing trend**. Unlike a Breaker Block (which sweeps liquidity first), a Mitigation Block fails to make a new swing extreme before price reverses through it and shifts market structure.

### Formation Sequence (unanimous across all sources)

**Bullish Mitigation Block:**
1. Market is in a bearish trend (series of LH and LL)
2. A bearish order block forms during the downtrend
3. Price reaches the bearish OB but **fails to make a new lower low**
4. Price reverses and **breaks above the prior lower high** (BOS / structure shift)
5. The failed bearish OB zone is now a **bullish mitigation block**
6. Entry: buy when price retests the MB zone

**Bearish Mitigation Block:**
1. Market is in a bullish trend (series of HH and HL)
2. A bullish order block forms during the uptrend
3. Price reaches the bullish OB but **fails to make a new higher high**
4. Price reverses and **breaks below the prior higher low** (BOS / structure shift)
5. The failed bullish OB zone is now a **bearish mitigation block**
6. Entry: sell when price retests the MB zone

### Key Distinction: Mitigation Block vs Breaker Block

| Aspect | Mitigation Block | Breaker Block |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Liquidity sweep | NO — fails to make new HH/LL | YES — makes new HH/LL first |
| OB failure type | OB fails before reaching prior extreme | OB fails AFTER sweeping beyond prior extreme |
| Structure shift | Required — BOS confirms the MB | Required — BOS confirms the breaker |
| Trade direction | Same — trade in direction of new structure | Same — trade in direction of new structure |

### Entry Rules
- **Zone:** The failed order block's high-to-low range
- **Entry type:** Limit order at the edge of the MB zone closest to trade direction
- **Confirmation required:** BOS or CHoCH must occur BEFORE the MB is valid
- **Displacement adds confluence:** An impulsive/displacement move away from the zone strengthens the setup

### Risk Management
- **Stop loss:** Beyond the opposite edge of the MB zone (beyond high for shorts, beyond low for longs)
- **Take profit:** Next liquidity target or opposing structure level
- **Minimum RR:** 1:2 recommended

## Validation of Existing Code

### FAIL — Major conceptual errors found:

1. **Wrong interpretation of "mitigation"**: The existing code treats mitigation as "a broken/penetrated OB becomes a re-entry zone." This is closer to a Breaker Block concept. An ICT Mitigation Block is specifically an OB that **fails to continue the trend** (no new HH/LL) before structure shifts.

2. **No structure shift (BOS) confirmation**: The code enters purely on price revisiting a mitigated zone, without confirming that a Break of Structure occurred. BOS is unanimously required across all sources.

3. **Penetration threshold is not an ICT concept**: The `mitigation_threshold` parameter (% of OB penetrated) is not part of ICT Mitigation Block theory. The qualifying event is structure shift, not zone penetration percentage.

4. **Wrong direction logic**: The code says "bearish OB mitigated = bullish re-entry." This accidentally gets the direction right for the wrong reason. The correct logic is: "bearish OB fails to push price lower, structure shifts bullish, MB becomes bullish re-entry."

5. **Missing displacement check**: No check for displacement/impulsive move away from the zone.

6. **Exit logic is arbitrary**: "2x zone size move against" is not an ICT concept. Exits should target opposing liquidity or structure levels.

**Verdict: REWRITE REQUIRED**
