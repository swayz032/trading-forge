## Consensus Rules (all sources agree)

### Session Times (New York / Eastern Time)
| Session | Start | End | Role |
|---------|-------|-----|------|
| Asia Range (setup) | 20:00 (8 PM) | 00:00 (midnight) | Builds the range; creates liquidity pools |
| London Killzone (execution) | 02:00 AM | 05:00 AM | Sweeps Asia range; provides the raid |

- All sources agree on Asia = 20:00-00:00 ET and London killzone = 02:00-05:00 ET.
- The 02:00-03:00 window is cited as the peak manipulation hour.

### Setup Sequence (unanimous across all sources)
1. **Mark Asia Range:** Record the high and low of the Asia session (20:00-00:00 ET).
2. **Wait for London Open:** At 02:00 ET, watch for price to move toward one side of the Asia range.
3. **Liquidity Sweep:** Price breaks above Asia high (sweeps BSL) or below Asia low (sweeps SSL). This is the "Judas Swing" — a false breakout designed to trigger stops.
4. **Displacement + MSS:** After the sweep, an aggressive move occurs in the OPPOSITE direction. This displacement must break a prior swing (Market Structure Shift) to confirm institutional intent.
5. **FVG Formation:** Within the displacement candles, a Fair Value Gap forms.
6. **Entry on FVG Retrace:** Enter when price retraces into the FVG zone.

### Direction Logic
- Asia LOW swept -> bullish setup (longs) — SSL taken, institutions buying
- Asia HIGH swept -> bearish setup (shorts) — BSL taken, institutions selling
- Trade goes AGAINST the direction of the sweep

### Risk Management (consensus)
- **Stop loss:** Beyond the sweep extreme (the Asia H/L that was swept, or the Judas Swing candle extreme)
- **Take profit (minimum):** Opposite side of the Asia range
- **Take profit (extended):** Prior day session liquidity levels (AM session H/L)
- **Session exit:** Exit before NY lunch (12:00 ET) if targets not reached — avoid dead zone

### Required Confirmations
- **Displacement:** YES — unanimously required. Must be an aggressive/impulsive candle.
- **Market Structure Shift:** YES — unanimously required. Displacement must break a prior swing.
- **FVG entry:** YES — consensus strongly favors FVG entry over raw displacement entry. FVG provides a specific zone and better risk:reward.
