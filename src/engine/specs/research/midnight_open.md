## Consensus Rules

1. **Exact time: 00:00 ET (midnight Eastern Time)** — all sources agree unanimously
2. **The level is the open price of the midnight candle** — the first tick/open of the 00:00 ET bar
3. **Primary use: daily bias reference** — price above MO = bullish intent, below = bearish intent
4. **Mean reversion / magnet effect** — price tends to retrace back to the MO during the NY session, making it a target level
5. **Institutional reference point** — banks and large players use this level for daily positioning decisions
6. **Intraday S/R** — the MO level acts as support when price is above and resistance when below
7. **Retracement window is 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET** — pre-market touches do not count in the classic framework

## Disputed Points

- **Midnight Open vs True Day Open (6:00 PM ET)**: Some sources conflate or confuse these. The True Day Open is when CME futures begin their new session (6 PM ET Sunday-Thursday). The Midnight Open is a separate level at 00:00 ET. ICT teaches both, but they serve different purposes. The MO is for intraday bias; the TDO is for broader daily framing.
- **Retracement probability reliability**: Edgeful provides specific backtested percentages (67-89% depending on day/instrument), but other sources state the concept qualitatively without hard numbers. The probabilities vary enough by instrument and weekday that blanket claims like "price always returns to MO" are overstated.
- **Whether MO is a trade entry or just a reference**: Some sources treat MO as an entry trigger (fade direction at open), while others (correctly per ICT) treat it as a reference level that informs bias but requires additional confluence (FVG, OB, etc.) for actual entry.

## Final Definition

**ICT Midnight Open** is the opening price at exactly **00:00 ET (midnight Eastern Time)**. It serves three functions:

1. **Daily Bias Gauge** — If the NY session (9:30 AM ET) opens above the MO, the day has bullish institutional intent. If below, bearish intent. This is the simplest and most universal application.

2. **Mean Reversion Target** — Price has a statistical tendency to retrace back to the MO level during the NY session. This makes MO a target for retracement trades, not necessarily an entry level itself.

3. **Institutional Positioning Reference** — Large players (banks, funds) use the MO as their "zero point" for the day. They accumulate longs below it and shorts above it, creating the magnet-like price behavior.

**Key distinction**: The Midnight Open (00:00 ET) is NOT the same as the True Day Open (6:00 PM ET / CME session open). Both are ICT concepts but serve different roles. The MO is specifically the intraday bias and retracement reference.

**How to use it**: Mark the 00:00 ET open price. At the NY session open, note whether price is above or below. If above, expect a retrace down to MO at some point (short bias for retracement); if below, expect a retrace up (long bias for retracement). Combine with other ICT tools (FVG, OB, killzone timing) for actual trade entries — the MO alone is a reference, not an entry signal.
