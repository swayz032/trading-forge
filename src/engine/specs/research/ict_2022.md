## Consensus Rules (agreed across all sources)

### 1. HTF Bias is REQUIRED (not optional)
- Every source lists daily bias as the **first step** before any execution
- Bias is determined on Daily or 4H timeframe
- Methods: liquidity sweep direction on Daily, premium/discount zone, HTF FVG/imbalance presence
- You only take longs in bullish bias, shorts in bearish bias — no exceptions

### 2. Liquidity Sweep MUST Precede MSS
- All 7 sources confirm the sweep happens BEFORE the MSS
- The sweep targets previous session highs/lows, previous day high/low, or other resting liquidity
- The sweep must align with the bias (sweep lows for bullish, sweep highs for bearish)
- Without a sweep, there is no valid setup

### 3. MSS Must Show Displacement
- MSS is not just any break of structure — it requires a **displacement move** (strong, impulsive candle(s))
- The displacement is what creates the FVG
- MSS is confirmed on LTF (5m, 3m, or 1m) after the sweep occurs on the range

### 4. FVG is the Primary Entry Mechanism
- After MSS + displacement, the FVG left behind is the entry zone
- Price must retrace back INTO the FVG for entry
- Other PD arrays (Order Block, Breaker Block, Inverse FVG) are also valid but FVG is the canonical entry

### 5. Killzone Timing is Mandatory
- The model only activates during specific sessions:
  - **London Killzone**: 02:00-05:00 ET (some sources say 03:00-05:00)
  - **New York Killzone**: 08:00-12:00 ET (futures: 08:00-11:30 ET)
- Setups outside killzones are not valid ICT 2022 model trades

### 6. Stop-Loss and Target Placement
- SL goes below the swept liquidity level (bullish) or above it (bearish)
- TP targets the opposing liquidity (e.g., previous day high for bullish, previous day low for bearish)
- Minimum 1:2 R:R required; model is designed for 1:3+

## Disputed / Variable Points

### OTE (0.618-0.786 Fib) — Required or Optional?
- **Consensus**: OTE is a **refinement tool**, not a hard requirement
- Sources 1, 2, 4, 6 describe OTE as "the best-risk entry INSIDE the FVG" — meaning FVG is the entry zone, OTE narrows it
- If the FVG itself falls within the OTE zone (62%-79% retracement of the displacement leg), that is the highest-conviction entry
- **Practical implication**: You can enter at the FVG without Fibonacci, but OTE improves the R:R. Not every FVG will align perfectly with OTE levels.

### Which PD Array for Entry?
- All sources agree FVG is the primary/canonical entry
- Sources 2, 3 mention Order Blocks, Breaker Blocks, and Inverse FVGs as alternative entry arrays
- Tradezella models explicitly include FVG as the named entry array in Models 3 and 4
- **Practical implication**: For codification, FVG should be the default; OB/BB/IFVG can be secondary filters

### Exact Killzone Hours
- London start varies: 02:00 ET vs 03:00 ET across sources
- NY end varies: 11:30 ET (futures) vs 12:00 ET (forex)
- **Practical implication**: Use 02:00-05:00 for London, 08:00-12:00 for NY as the widest valid windows; can tighten per instrument

### Midnight Open Range
- Sources 1, 3, 6 specifically mention marking the range from NY midnight (00:00 ET) to London open (03:00 ET) as the initial range to sweep
- Sources 2, 5, 7 discuss previous day high/low or session high/low more generically
- **Practical implication**: Both are valid liquidity pools; midnight range is more specific to the 2022 mentorship

## Final Definition

### ICT 2022 Model — Canonical Sequence

```
PHASE 0: PREPARATION
  - Determine Daily Bias (Daily/4H TF)
    - Which side was liquidity last swept on Daily?
    - Is price in premium or discount zone on HTF?
    - Is there a HTF FVG/imbalance drawing price?
  - Mark key liquidity levels:
    - Previous Day High (PDH) / Previous Day Low (PDL)
    - Midnight Open Range (00:00-03:00 ET high/low)
    - Session highs/lows

PHASE 1: LIQUIDITY SWEEP (during Killzone)
  - Bullish bias: price sweeps below a key low (PDL, session low, midnight range low)
  - Bearish bias: price sweeps above a key high (PDH, session high, midnight range high)
  - The sweep collects resting stop orders / triggers liquidity

PHASE 2: MARKET STRUCTURE SHIFT (MSS) + DISPLACEMENT
  - On LTF (5m/3m/1m), price breaks a recent swing high (bullish) or swing low (bearish)
  - The break must occur with DISPLACEMENT — strong, impulsive candle(s)
  - This displacement creates a Fair Value Gap (FVG)

PHASE 3: ENTRY
  - Wait for price to retrace INTO the FVG left by the displacement
  - Optimal: FVG overlaps with OTE zone (62%-79% Fibonacci retracement of displacement leg)
  - Entry trigger: price touches the FVG
  - Alternative PD arrays: Order Block, Breaker Block, Inverse FVG (secondary)

PHASE 4: TRADE MANAGEMENT
  - Stop-Loss: below swept low (bullish) / above swept high (bearish)
  - Take-Profit: opposing liquidity (PDH for longs, PDL for shorts) or next HTF PD array
  - Minimum R:R: 1:2 (target 1:3+)
  - Max risk: 1% of account per trade
```
