## Consensus Rules (all sources agree)

### Session-to-Phase Mapping (Daily PO3)
| Phase | Session | Time (ET) | Description |
|-------|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Accumulation | Asia | 20:00-00:00 | Consolidation range; liquidity builds above and below |
| Manipulation | London Open | 02:00-05:00 | Judas Swing sweeps one side of Asia range (false breakout) |
| Distribution | NY AM | 08:30-11:00 | Real directional move opposite to the London sweep |

- All sources agree: Asia = Accumulation, London = Manipulation, NY = Distribution
- The manipulation is specifically called a "Judas Swing" — a deceptive move that sweeps liquidity

### Setup Sequence (unanimous)
1. **Accumulation:** During Asia session, price builds a consolidation range. Mark the Asia High and Asia Low.
2. **Manipulation (Judas Swing):** During London open, price sweeps one side of the Asia range:
   - Sweeps Asia High = bearish Judas Swing (look for shorts in NY)
   - Sweeps Asia Low = bullish Judas Swing (look for longs in NY)
3. **Confirmation:** After the sweep, displacement + MSS must occur in the OPPOSITE direction to the sweep. FVG forms within the displacement candles.
4. **Distribution (Entry):** During NY AM session, enter on FVG retrace in the direction opposite to the sweep.

### Direction Logic
- Asia High swept during London -> SHORT during NY (the high sweep was fake, real move is down)
- Asia Low swept during London -> LONG during NY (the low sweep was fake, real move is up)
- Trade direction = OPPOSITE of the London sweep direction

### Confirmation Requirements
- **Displacement:** Required by 5/6 sources explicitly. The move after the sweep must be aggressive/impulsive.
- **MSS/CHoCH:** Market Structure Shift confirms the reversal is legitimate (not just a pullback).
- **FVG:** Fair Value Gap forms within the displacement move; entry on FVG retrace is the highest-confidence entry model.
- **Minimum confirmation stack:** Sweep + Displacement (4/6 sources say this is sufficient)
- **Full confirmation stack:** Sweep + Displacement + MSS + FVG (2/6 sources require all four)

### Risk Management
- **Stop loss:** Beyond the manipulation extreme (below the sweep low for longs, above the sweep high for shorts)
- **Take profit:** Opposing liquidity pool, previous session high/low, or opposing FVG edge
- **Minimum RR:** 2:1 (unanimous)
- **Session exit:** Close position by end of NY PM (15:45 ET) if target not hit — this is an intraday strategy

### Exit Rules
- **Primary exit:** Take profit at opposing liquidity (PDH/PDL, prior session extremes)
- **Time exit:** Close by 15:45 ET if target not hit (day-only strategy)
- **Structure exit:** If market structure breaks against your position during distribution, exit immediately
- **Max bars held:** ~20 bars on 5m (approx 100 minutes) — if trade hasn't worked in that timeframe, the setup likely failed
