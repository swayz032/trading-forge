## Consensus Rules

All 6 sources agree on the following:

1. **SMT requires TWO correlated instruments.** This is non-negotiable. The entire concept is built on comparing the swing structure of two assets that normally move together (positive correlation) or inversely (negative correlation). A single-chart indicator divergence is a completely different concept.

2. **Divergence definition.** One instrument makes a new swing high/low while the correlated instrument fails to confirm that same move. Specifically:
   - **Bearish SMT:** Instrument A prints a higher high; Instrument B prints a lower high (or equal high). Signals potential reversal down.
   - **Bullish SMT:** Instrument A prints a lower low; Instrument B prints a higher low (or equal low). Signals potential reversal up.

3. **SMT is confirmation, not entry.** Every source states SMT divergence should not be used as a standalone entry trigger. It confirms a thesis formed by other ICT tools (PD Arrays, OBs, FVGs, liquidity sweeps).

4. **Location matters.** SMT divergence is only meaningful when it occurs at a higher-timeframe point of interest — an order block, FVG, liquidity pool, or premium/discount zone.

5. **Structural confirmation is required after divergence.** After spotting SMT divergence, wait for:
   - MSS (Market Structure Shift) — a decisive break of a significant swing point with displacement
   - CHoCH (Change of Character) — equivalent concept, break against prior regime
   - BOS (Break of Structure) — confirms directional commitment

6. **Recommended instrument pairs:**

   | Market   | Positive Correlation Pairs          | Inverse Correlation         |
   |----------|-------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
   | Indices  | ES vs NQ, ES vs YM, NQ vs YM       | Indices vs DXY              |
   | Forex    | EUR/USD vs GBP/USD                  | EUR/USD vs DXY, GBP/USD vs DXY |
   | Metals   | Gold (XAU) vs Silver (XAG)          | Gold vs DXY                 |
   | Crypto   | BTC vs ETH                          | —                           |

7. **Optimal timeframe:** 15-minute or lower for entry-level SMT detection. Higher timeframes for context/bias.

## Disputed Points

### RSI Divergence as Substitute
- **One source (icttrading.org)** conflates SMT with traditional oscillator divergence, describing it as "price vs RSI/MACD mismatch." This appears to be an error or oversimplification — it contradicts every other source.
- **All other sources (5/6)** explicitly define SMT as cross-instrument comparison, not single-chart indicator divergence. RSI/MACD divergence is a fundamentally different concept measuring momentum decay on a single instrument.
- **Verdict:** RSI divergence is NOT a valid substitute for SMT. They measure different things. RSI divergence can be used as *additional* confluence alongside SMT, but cannot replace the cross-instrument comparison.

### Which Instrument Leads
- Some sources imply NQ leads ES (tech leads broad market). Others treat both instruments symmetrically.
- No strong consensus on which instrument's failure is more significant.

### Exact Swing Definition
- Sources vary on whether swings must be defined by a specific number of candles (e.g., 3-candle swing) or left to discretion.
- No strict consensus on the lookback window for identifying the "previous" swing high/low.

## Final Definition

**ICT SMT (Smart Money Technique) Reversal** is a cross-instrument divergence signal that identifies potential trend reversals by comparing the swing structure of two correlated assets.

### Setup Conditions
1. Identify two correlated instruments (e.g., ES and NQ for index futures).
2. Both instruments must be at or near a higher-timeframe PD Array (order block, FVG, liquidity pool).
3. On 15-minute or lower timeframe, observe swing highs/lows on both instruments.

### Signal
- **Bearish SMT:** Instrument A makes a higher high while Instrument B makes a lower high (fails to confirm). This indicates smart money distribution — the rally is not supported across correlated markets.
- **Bullish SMT:** Instrument A makes a lower low while Instrument B makes a higher low (fails to confirm). This indicates smart money accumulation — the sell-off is not supported across correlated markets.

### Confirmation
After SMT divergence is detected, wait for a Market Structure Shift (MSS) — a decisive break of a swing point on the instrument you intend to trade. Entry is taken on the MSS displacement, typically into the resulting FVG.

### What SMT Is NOT
- It is NOT single-instrument RSI/MACD divergence.
- It is NOT a standalone entry signal.
- It is NOT valid in isolation — requires location (PD Array) and confirmation (MSS/CHoCH).

### Canonical Implementation for Futures
For ES/NQ futures trading:
1. Identify daily/4H bias using higher-timeframe PD Arrays.
2. On 15m or 5m, compare ES and NQ swing structure at the PD Array.
3. If one index makes a new extreme and the other fails to confirm — SMT divergence is present.
4. Wait for MSS on the trading instrument.
5. Enter on displacement into the FVG created by the MSS candle.
6. Stop above/below the SMT swing point. Target: opposing liquidity.
