# ICT Optimal Trade Entry (OTE) — Cross-Validation Report

## Sources

1. **TradingFinder** (tradingfinder.com) — OTE zone is between 0.618 and 0.79 Fibonacci retracement of the impulse swing. The precise OTE level is 0.705. Must identify trend on higher timeframes first, then locate most recent significant swing high/low. OTEs are more effective in trending markets (higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows established). Draw Fib from swing low to swing high (bullish) or swing high to swing low (bearish).

2. **FXNX** (fxnx.com) — OTE sweet spot is 0.705, the mathematical midpoint between 0.618 and 0.79. Before drawing the Fib, a Market Structure Shift (MSS) must be observed (previous swing high/low broken with force). The displacement leg must show large-bodied candles with little wicking (institutional momentum). Anchor the Fib at the absolute start of the displacement move to where price begins to stall. When an FVG sits inside the OTE zone, that is the ideal confluence entry. OTE is the "discount/premium address"; PD Arrays (OB/FVG) are the "front door" within it.

3. **InnerCircleTrader.net** — OTE drawn from the swing leg that produced the break of structure. Entry between 61.8%--79% retracement of that leg. 0.705 is the optimal level. Displacement (impulsive price delivery) must precede the retracement. Entering at 0.705 aligns with institutional order flow — deeper than average retail entries but still within the zone of institutional sponsorship. Stop loss placed beyond the swing high/low of the measured leg.

4. **Forex Factory / TFlab** — Comprehensive OTE requires: (a) higher-timeframe directional bias, (b) displacement creating BOS/MSS, (c) retracement into the 0.618--0.79 zone, (d) PD array (FVG or OB) within that zone for precision entry. The OTE zone alone is not the trigger — a PD array inside it is.

5. **HowToTrade.com** — OTE works best in trending conditions. Sideways/ranging markets reduce reliability. Fib is drawn on the most recent impulsive swing that broke structure. Confirmation of reversal within the OTE zone (e.g., rejection candle, FVG respect) strengthens the setup.

## Consensus Rules

- **Fib levels**: The OTE zone spans **0.618 to 0.79** retracement of the impulse leg; **0.705** is the sweet spot.
- **Swing leg**: Measure the most recent **displacement leg** — the impulsive move that created a Break of Structure (BOS) or Market Structure Shift (MSS). Large-bodied candles, minimal wicks.
- **BOS/MSS required**: All sources agree that a structural break must precede the OTE setup. Without a confirmed MSS/BOS, there is no valid displacement leg to measure.
- **Trending context**: OTE is a trend-continuation entry. Higher-timeframe directional bias must be established first.
- **Stop placement**: Beyond the high/low of the displacement swing (the 0.0 or 1.0 Fib anchor).

## Disputed Points

- **Is an FVG required at OTE?** Sources 2 and 4 (FXNX, TFlab) strongly recommend an FVG or OB sitting inside the OTE zone as the actual execution trigger (confluence). Sources 1 and 5 (TradingFinder, HowToTrade) describe the OTE zone itself as sufficient for entry, with PD arrays being optional enhancement. Source 3 (InnerCircleTrader.net) sits in the middle — displacement implies FVGs will be present but does not make them an explicit requirement.
- **0.618 vs 0.705 as the zone start**: Some sources define the OTE zone as 0.618--0.79, while others narrow it to 0.705--0.79. The broader range is more commonly cited, with 0.705 being the preferred level within it.
- **Fib direction convention**: Minor inconsistency in whether the Fib is drawn high-to-low or low-to-high. The functional rule is the same: the OTE zone is the 61.8%--79% retracement depth of the impulse move, regardless of drawing direction.

## Final Definition

**ICT OTE (Optimal Trade Entry)** is a Fibonacci-based entry technique for entering trades in the direction of the prevailing trend after a confirmed structural break.

**Setup conditions:**
1. **Higher-timeframe bias** established (trending market, not ranging).
2. **Displacement leg** identified — an impulsive move (large bodies, minimal wicks) that produces a **Break of Structure (BOS)** or **Market Structure Shift (MSS)**.
3. **Fibonacci retracement** drawn from the start of the displacement leg to its terminus.
4. **OTE zone** = the **0.618 to 0.79** retracement band. The **0.705** level is the sweet spot.
5. **PD array confluence** (recommended) — an FVG or Order Block sitting inside the OTE zone provides the precision entry trigger.

**Execution:**
- Enter at or near 0.705 (or when price reaches a PD array within the OTE zone).
- Stop loss beyond the displacement leg's origin (the 0% or 100% Fib level, depending on direction).
- Target: next liquidity pool, opposing PD array, or measured extension.
