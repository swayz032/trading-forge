## Consensus Rules

All 6 sources agree on these points:

1. **Definition**: The Judas Swing is a deliberate false move at the start of a session designed to trap retail traders in the wrong direction before the real move begins.

2. **Primary session**: London Open window. The highest-probability window is **02:00-05:00 EST** (07:00-10:00 UTC). The reference price is the **New York Midnight Open (00:00 EST)**.

3. **Mechanism -- AMD cycle**:
   - **A** (Accumulation): Asian session consolidation builds liquidity on both sides of the range
   - **M** (Manipulation): The Judas Swing sweeps one side of that liquidity (the fake move)
   - **D** (Distribution): The real move in the opposite direction

4. **Direction logic**:
   - Bullish day -> Judas Swing moves **down** (sweeps sell-side liquidity / Asian lows)
   - Bearish day -> Judas Swing moves **up** (sweeps buy-side liquidity / Asian highs)

5. **Confirmation requires two things**:
   - **(a) Liquidity sweep**: Price must clearly run beyond a defined level (Asian range high/low, previous session high/low)
   - **(b) Market Structure Shift (MSS)**: Price must aggressively reverse and break the last swing point that created the liquidity grab. Must show displacement (large body candle), not a weak wick.

6. **Entry mechanism**:
   - After MSS, drop to lower time frame (1m-3m)
   - Wait for price to retrace into the FVG, Order Block, or Breaker Block created by the displacement
   - Enter in the **opposite direction** of the fake move
   - Stop loss below/above the Judas Swing extreme (the manipulation wick)
   - Target: opposing liquidity pool (previous highs if long, previous lows if short)

7. **Prerequisite**: You need a **Daily Bias** (HTF directional lean) before trading this setup. The Judas Swing is a timing/entry model, not a directional model.

8. **Time frames**: Identify the setup on 15m-1H, refine entry on 1m-5m.

## Final Definition

**The ICT Judas Swing** is a false opening-session price move engineered by institutional order flow to sweep resting liquidity before the true directional move begins. It is the "Manipulation" phase of the AMD (Accumulation-Manipulation-Distribution) cycle.

### Setup Conditions
1. **Daily Bias established** -- HTF analysis (Daily/4H) indicates expected direction for the day
2. **Asian session consolidation** -- Price ranges between 20:00 and 00:00 EST, building liquidity on both sides
3. **Reference price** -- Mark the New York Midnight Open (00:00 EST)

### Identification (02:00-05:00 EST primary window)
4. **Fake move begins** -- Price breaks aggressively through the Asian range in the direction **opposite** to your daily bias
5. **Liquidity sweep confirmed** -- The move clearly runs beyond a defined level (Asian high/low, previous session high/low, equal highs/lows)
6. **Market Structure Shift (MSS)** -- Price reverses with displacement (large-bodied candle) and breaks the swing point that created the sweep. This confirms the manipulation is over.

### Entry (opposite direction of fake move)
7. **Drop to LTF** (1m-5m) after MSS
8. **Wait for retracement** into the FVG or Order Block left by the displacement candle
9. **Enter** in the direction of your daily bias (opposite to the Judas Swing)
10. **Stop loss** -- Below/above the Judas Swing extreme wick + 5-20 pip buffer
11. **Target** -- Opposing liquidity pool (buy-side liquidity if long, sell-side if short)

### Session Variants
- **London Judas Swing**: 02:00-05:00 EST (highest probability, most documented)
- **NY AM Judas Swing**: 09:30-09:45 EST (equity/index variant, sweeps London session range)

### Risk Notes
- Without a correct daily bias, the "Judas Swing" may actually be the real move -- bias is the prerequisite filter
- Not every Asian range sweep is a Judas Swing -- MSS with displacement is the mandatory confirmation
- Works best on liquid pairs/instruments: EUR/USD, GBP/USD, NAS100, XAUUSD
